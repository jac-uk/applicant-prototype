092 Registrar of Criminal Appeals, Master of the Crown Office and Queen’s Coroner & Attorney

Closing date: 13:00 Wednesday 11 October 2019

Number of vacancies: 1

Location: Location: Royal Courts of Justice, London

Appointment type: Full time, but 50-50 job share would be considered

Salary: Group 6.1 - £134, 841

Overview of the role

As Registrar of Criminal Appeals the post holder has responsibility for ensuring that applications and appeals received by the Court of Appeal Criminal Division are placed before the court in good time in accordance with the statutory powers conferred on them. In exercising these powers, the Registrar must be able to demonstrate a deep understanding of criminal law, procedure and sentencing.

The role entails providing judicial leadership to the legal and administrative staff of the Criminal Appeal Office; exercising their duty of active case management by giving directions for the progression of cases; holding directions hearings in open court; identifying, on a termly basis, which judges of the Court of Appeal are to sit in the Criminal Division; and liaising with, and seeking the direction of the Lord Chief Justice, the Vice President and other senior judges of the court. As Registrar of the Court Martial Appeal Court the post holder will exercise similar functions in relation to that court.

As Queen’s Coroner and Attorney and Master of the Crown Office the post holder will undertake such interlocutory work of the Administrative Court as may be assigned to them by rule or practice or direction of the lead judge of that Court.

Location and jurisdiction

There are 50 vacancies in all regions in England and Wales. Successful candidates will be assigned to a particular region after discussion with the President of Employment Tribunals for England and Wales. Welsh language is required for up to 3 vacancies in the Employment Tribunal.

Successful candidates should be flexible and be willing to travel to various hearing venues as and when required to meet business need. These locations will be, as far as possible, near to candidates' homes and offices but on occasion they may be required to sit further afield.
