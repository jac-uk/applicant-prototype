Overview of the role

As Registrar of Criminal Appeals the post holder has responsibility for ensuring that applications and appeals received by the Court of Appeal Criminal Division are placed before the court in good time in accordance with the statutory powers conferred on them.

In exercising these powers, the Registrar must be able to demonstrate a deep understanding of criminal law, procedure and sentencing. The role entails providing judicial leadership to the legal and administrative staff of the Criminal Appeal Office; exercising their duty of active case management by giving directions for the progression of cases; holding directions hearings in open court; identifying, on a termly basis, which judges of the Court of Appeal are to sit in the Criminal Division; and liaising with, and seeking the direction of the Lord Chief Justice, the Vice President and other senior judges of the court. As Registrar of the Court Martial Appeal Court the post holder will exercise similar functions in relation to that court.
