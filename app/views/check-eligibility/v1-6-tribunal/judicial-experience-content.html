Previous judicial experience

For some posts, you'll be expected to have previous judicial experience.

This means experience of sitting as a judge, or a similar role for a ‘quasi-judicial body’ for which a legal qualification is required.

Quasi-judicial bodies have powers and procedures similar to those of courts. For example, determining facts and making decisions.

Examples of quasi-judicial bodies include:

  • Coroner
  • Parole Board
  • Chair of a statutory inquiry

It’s expected that you’ll have at least 30 sitting days in this role, not including training or sick days.

In exceptional cases, we might accept that you’ve gained the necessary skills in some other significant way.
