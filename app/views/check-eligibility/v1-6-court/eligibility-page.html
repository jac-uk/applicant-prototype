092 Registrar of Criminal Appeals, Master of the Crown Office and Queen’s Coroner & Attorney

Check if you're eligible

If you cannot meet all the requirements, you can still apply but it could mean you’re unlikely to be offered a position.

Citizenship

You're a citizen of the UK, the Republic of Ireland or another Commonwealth country.

Character

There are no questions about your character that could affect your suitability for this role. Criminal, motoring and financial offences, and professional misconduct or negligence could all affect your suitability for this role.

Reasonable length of service

You can work for at least 5 years before reaching the retirement age of 70.

Qualifications

You qualified in England or Wales as a barrister, fellow of CILEx or solicitor.

Or, you qualified in Northern Ireland or Scotland as a barrister or solicitor and have completed the Qualified Lawyers Training Scheme (QLTS).

Post-qualifying experience

You have at least 5 years of post-qualification law-related work experience.

Additional eligibility requirements

You have experience of criminal law and procedure in the Crown Court and/or the Court of Appeal, Criminal Division that includes sufficient directly relevant previous judicial experience.
